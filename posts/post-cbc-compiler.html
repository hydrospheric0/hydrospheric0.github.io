cbc-compiler

Published: March 10, 2026

The Audubon Christmas Bird Count (CBC) is the longest-running community science project in the world. Each winter, thousands of volunteers fan out across designated 15-mile-diameter circles to tally every bird they find. Compiling the results — merging dozens of handwritten field sheets, reconciling taxonomic names, computing sector summaries, and formatting the final report — is tedious and error-prone when done by hand.

cbc-compiler is a browser-based tool that automates that workflow. You upload the raw field-sheet data (CSV or tab-delimited), and the app merges records, flags duplicates and inconsistencies, tallies species by sector, and produces a formatted summary ready for submission to Audubon.

The interface runs entirely in the browser — no server, no login, no data leaves your machine. It is built with vanilla JavaScript and tested against several years of real CBC circle data.

Try it out at cbc-compiler.

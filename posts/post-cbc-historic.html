CBC historic

Published: February 24, 2026

The Audubon Christmas Bird Count has been running since 1900, producing one of the richest long-term ecological datasets in North America. Yet accessing the historical results — buried in PDFs, scanned tables, and legacy databases — has never been straightforward.

CBC historic is a browser-based tool that makes decades of CBC circle data searchable and browsable. Pick a circle and a date range, and the app surfaces species lists, party-hour effort, and participant counts in a clean, sortable interface. It is designed so that compilers and researchers can quickly answer questions like “When was species X last recorded here?” or “How has observer effort changed over the years?”

Everything runs client-side — no server, no account required. The data is fetched directly from publicly available CBC results and rendered with vanilla JavaScript.

Try it out at CBC historic.
